We’ve all been there. You take a loss. It was a "perfect" setup, but it hit your stop loss anyway. You feel a surge of anger. You think, "That was market manipulation. The broker is hunting my stops. I'm going to take my money back."

Five minutes later, you’ve opened a position twice as large in the opposite direction. No plan. No analysis. Just pure, unadulterated revenge.

This is "Revenge Trading." And it is the #1 killer of trading careers. Let’s look at the cold, hard numbers of why it’s mathematically impossible to win this way.

## The Mathematical Spiral

Imagine you have a £10,000 account and you follow the 1% rule. You take a loss.
*   **Balance:** £9,900.
*   **Psychology:** "Annoying, but my edge will play out over the next 10 trades."

Now imagine you Revenge Trade. You’re angry, so you risk 5% to "make it back fast." You lose again.
*   **Balance:** £9,405.
*   **Psychology:** Panic. You now need a 6.3% return just to get back to where you started.

Because you’re panicking, you risk 10% on the next trade. You lose again.
*   **Balance:** £8,464.
*   **Psychology:** Desperation. You now need an 18% return to break even.

In three trades, you have put yourself in a position that will take months of perfect trading to fix. All because you couldn't accept a £100 loss.

## The "Double Down" Fallacy

Revenge trading often involves "averaging down"—buying more as the price goes against you. 

The logic is: "If I buy at 100, and it drops to 90, I'll buy more. Then if it bounces to 95, I'm at break-even!"

But what if it doesn't bounce? What if it goes to 80? Now you have a massive position that is hemorrhaging money. You aren't trading anymore; you are praying. And the market doesn't listen to prayers.

## The Opportunity Cost

The biggest cost of revenge trading isn't the money. It's the **Time.**

When you're stuck in a "revenge" cycle, you aren't looking for good setups. You are blinded by your previous loss. You miss the actual high-probability trades because you're too busy fighting a losing battle.

## How to Stop the Cycle

I had to blow two accounts before I learned how to stop. Here is my "Circuit Breaker" protocol:

1.  **The Two-Loss Rule:** If I take two losses in a single day, I am done. I close the laptop and I do not open it until tomorrow.
2.  **The Physical Reset:** After a loss, I literally stand up and walk out of the room. I need to break the "screen-stare" loop.
3.  **Journal the Feeling:** I write down exactly how I feel. "I am angry because I lost £100." Seeing it on paper makes it look small and ridiculous.

## Final Word

The market will always be there. Your capital is the only thing that is finite. 

If you take a loss, say "thank you" for the lesson, and move on. The second you try to "get even" with the market, you have already lost.

### The Revenge Trading Red Flags
*   Increasing position size after a loss.
*   Removing stop losses.
*   Trading outside of your scheduled hours.
*   Feeling a "racing heart" or physical anger.
