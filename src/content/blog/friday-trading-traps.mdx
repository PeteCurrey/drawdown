It’s 3:00 PM on a Friday. You’ve had a great week. You’re up 3%. You’re feeling good. You think, "One more quick trade and I can finish the week at 4%."

By 5:00 PM, you aren't up 4%. You're down 2%. You've given back your entire week's profit and then some. You spend the weekend miserable, dreading the Monday open.

This is the "Friday Trap." And it is entirely predictable.

## The Psychology of the "Finish Line"

In our minds, Friday is the end of the game. We want to finish on a high. This creates a psychological pressure to "force" a trade if the day hasn't been profitable, or to "squeeze" more out of the market if it has.

This is **Result-Oriented Thinking**, and it is the enemy of discipline. The market doesn't know it's Friday. It doesn't care about your weekly target. It is just moving.

## Institutional Profit Taking

The "Big Money"—the banks and hedge funds—often close their positions before the weekend to avoid "Gap Risk" (when the market opens at a different price on Monday due to weekend news).

This creates two things on a Friday afternoon:
1.  **Strange Volatility:** Massive moves that don't seem to follow any technical logic.
2.  **Low Liquidity:** As the big players leave, the "spreads" can widen, making it more expensive to trade.

## The "Revenge Trading" Spiral

If you lose money on a Friday morning, the panic is twice as strong. You know that if you don't make it back today, you have to wait 48 hours for another chance. This leads to bigger position sizes, tighter stops, and eventual disaster.

## My Friday Rules

I developed these rules after losing way too many "Friday finishes."

1.  **No New Trades after 2:00 PM GMT.** The market gets too unpredictable as the London close approaches.
2.  **If you're in profit for the week, PROTECT IT.** Don't risk your weekly gain for a Friday "gamble."
3.  **Check the "Gap Risk."** If you're holding a trade over the weekend, you are exposed to whatever happens in the world on Saturday and Sunday. I usually close everything by Friday night.

## Final Word

Your goal as a trader is to be consistent over years, not days. If you find yourself consistently losing money on Fridays, the solution is simple: **Stop trading on Fridays.**

The market will still be there on Monday. Your capital might not be if you don't learn to walk away.
